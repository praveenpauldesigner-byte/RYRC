Part 1 · The Word

What Does It Mean to Remember?

To remember means to keep something in our mind so that we can recall it when needed — not a passing thought, but a deep knowing we live by daily.

Example 1: A Shopping List

Suppose your mother gives you a list of 10 items to purchase. Without writing them down, you may remember only 8 and forget 2. To avoid forgetting, we use external memory tools:

  • Pen and paper
  • Notes app on a phone
  • Reminder applications
  • Checklists
Example 2: Personal Info You Never Forget

It is usually difficult to forget:

  • Your birthday
  • Your phone number
  • Your home address
  • Your mobile unlock pattern
  • Your laptop password
Why Do We Remember These Things?
🔁

We Use Them Repeatedly

Frequent use strengthens memory. Daily time with God builds a lifetime habit.

❤️

They Are Emotionally Connected

Emotionally meaningful information is easier to remember and hold onto.

⭐

We Consider Them Important

We naturally prioritise remembering what we believe is valuable and necessary.

💡

Key Insight: The things we repeatedly focus on, emotionally value, and consider important are the things we remember most easily.

Who Is Truly the Creator?

The one true Creator is God — the eternal, self-existing being who brought all things into existence out of nothing (ex nihilo). In the Bible, this Creator is identified as Yahweh (the LORD), the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, fully revealed through Jesus Christ.

"In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth."

— Genesis 1:1

"For by Him all things were created, in heaven and on earth."

— Colossians 1:16 (referring to Christ)
How Can We Define a Creator?

A Creator is someone who brings something into existence from nothing, has original power and authority over what is made, designs with purpose and intention, sustains what has been created, and exists independently of the creation.

An engineer designs a machine. An artist creates a painting. A builder constructs a house. However, all human creators work with materials that already exist. God alone created the universe itself — humans can make or craft, but only God truly creates from nothing.

The 7 Characteristics of a Creator
♾️

1. Eternal & Self-Existing

No beginning or end — He existed before creation itself. "From everlasting to everlasting, you are God." — Ps 90:2

⚡

2. All-Powerful (Omnipotent)

Creates by the power of His word alone. "God said, 'Let there be light,' and there was light." — Gen 1:3

🧠

3. All-Knowing (Omniscient)

Designed creation with perfect wisdom and knowledge. "In wisdom you made them all." — Psalm 104:24

🌍

4. All-Present (Omnipresent)

Fills and sustains all of creation. "Do I not fill heaven and earth?" — Jeremiah 23:24

🕊️

5. Holy & Perfect

Morally pure and without flaw. "Holy, holy, holy is the LORD Almighty." — Isaiah 6:3

🎯

6. Purposeful

Everything He creates has meaning. "We are God's handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works." — Eph 2:10

🏔️

7. Independent (Self-Sufficient)

Needs nothing outside Himself. "The God who made the world… is not served by human hands, as if he needed anything." — Acts 17:24–25

What a True Creator Is NOT
  • Dependent — God does not need creation to exist
  • Finite — God is not limited by time, space, or ability
  • Changeable — God does not grow, age, or evolve
  • Ignorant — God is never unaware or surprised
  • Weak — God's power is never exhausted
  • Sinful — God cannot act against His own holy nature
  • Created — God was never made by another; He always was

Part 4 · The Science

Your Brain Is Still Being Built

The prefrontal cortex (PFC) is the front part of the brain, located just behind the forehead. It is the last part of the brain to fully develop, completing its maturation around age 25.

What the Prefrontal Cortex Controls
FunctionWhat It Means Practically
Decision MakingThinking before acting
Impulse ControlResisting temptation
Emotional RegulationManaging anger, fear, excitement
Moral ReasoningDistinguishing right from wrong
Planning & Goal SettingThinking about future consequences
Social BehaviourUnderstanding others' feelings
Risk AssessmentEvaluating danger before acting
Why This Matters for Youth

Because the prefrontal cortex is still developing, young people are:

  • More likely to take risks
  • More influenced by peer pressure
  • More driven by emotions than logic
  • More vulnerable to addiction, bad habits, and wrong decisions

But also more teachable, moldable, and open to lasting change — exactly why the habits, values, and faith formed during youth tend to last a lifetime.

— The greatest window of transformation
Part 5 · The Stakes

Why Youth Is Your Superpower

🏗️

1. The Foundation Stage

Everything built in youth becomes the base of adulthood. Bad foundations lead to unstable lives. Strong foundations lead to resilient futures.

🧬

2. Maximum Neuroplasticity

The young brain has the highest capacity for learning, habit formation, and change. Languages, disciplines, and faith planted young take deepest root.

⚡

3. Peak Energy & Potential

Youth carries the greatest physical strength, mental sharpness, and emotional passion — resources that diminish with age.

🪞

4. Identity Is Being Formed

Youth is when you answer: Who am I? What do I believe? What is my purpose? Who do I want to become?

📅

5. Habits Are Established

Most lifelong habits — spiritual disciplines, work ethic, relationship patterns, addictions, character traits — are formed before age 25.

🌱

6. Greatest Influence Window

Youth are most open to mentorship, discipleship, and transformation before life's responsibilities narrow their focus.

Part 7 · The Biblical Call

Why Should Youth Be Dedicated to God?

1. God Specifically Calls the Young

"Remember your Creator in the days of your youth, before the evil days come and the years approach when you will say, 'I find no pleasure in them.'"

— Ecclesiastes 12:1

This is a direct, urgent command — remember God NOW, while you are young, before: life becomes heavy with burdens, bad habits become chains, the heart grows hard, or opportunities pass away.

2. Youth Sets the Direction of the Whole Life

"Start children off on the way they should go, and even when they are old they will not turn from it."

— Proverbs 22:6

The direction chosen in youth determines the destination of life.

5. Youthful Dedication Produces Lifelong Fruitfulness

"The righteous will flourish like a palm tree… They will still bear fruit in old age."

— Psalm 92:12, 14

Those who give their youth to God live lives of lasting purpose, bearing fruit even into old age.

6. Your Body Is God's Temple

"Do you not know that your bodies are temples of the Holy Spirit… Therefore honor God with your bodies."

— 1 Corinthians 6:19–20

Youth is when the body is strongest and most active — all the more reason to consecrate it to God's purposes rather than waste it on sin.

3. God Uses Young People for Great Purposes

God called and used ordinary young people mightily throughout history — and He is still doing so today.

4. The Enemy Targets Youth First

Satan knows that capturing youth means capturing the future. He specifically uses:

Watch Out For These Traps

  • Lust and sexual immorality
  • Pride and rebellion
  • Substance abuse and addiction
  • Distraction and laziness
  • Wrong friendships and peer pressure

"Flee the evil desires of youth and pursue righteousness, faith, love and peace."

— 2 Timothy 2:22
🔥

A Prayer for the Young

Dear Lord Jesus,

I believe that You are my Creator and my Savior. Thank You for the gift of life, the strength of youth, and the opportunity to know You more each day.

Help me remember You in the days of my youth and keep my heart from the distractions of this world. Separate me from anything that pulls me away from Your presence, Your truth, and Your purpose for my life.

Teach me to trust in You rather than in my own strength. Lead me, guide me, and transform me daily. Help me abide in You, grow in faith, and become more like You in my thoughts, words, and actions.

Lord, thank You for saving me and giving me a future filled with hope. Align my heart with Your will and use my life for Your glory. Let Your light shine through me so that others may see You in me.

Fill me with Your Holy Spirit, strengthen me to walk faithfully with You, and help me live each day according to Your purpose.

Thank You for Your unfailing love, grace, and mercy.

In Jesus’ name, Amen.
